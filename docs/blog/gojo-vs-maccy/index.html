Gojo vs Maccy: clipboard history or a broader Mac utility?

Maccy is a dedicated keyboard-first clipboard manager. Gojo includes clipboard history as one part of a broader notch workspace.

Updated July 7, 20266 min readAll posts

Short answer

Choose Maccy if you want a focused clipboard-history app with search, keyboard-first use, pinned items, and local storage. Choose Gojo if clipboard history is one of several tools you want to access from a broader notch workspace.

  • Best specialist fit: focused clipboard history with search, pinned items, and keyboard-first recall.
  • Best Gojo fit: clipboard history as one part of a wider MacBook notch utility.
  • Choose by scope: dedicated clipboard manager or a multi-tool notch surface.

Where each app fits

Where Maccy is stronger

Maccy is stronger when clipboard history is the job. Its official positioning is keyboard-first search and reuse, with a native, minimal interface and local storage.

Where Gojo is stronger

Gojo is stronger when clipboard access belongs beside other everyday controls. It puts clipboard history near media, files, windows, calendar, battery, webcam mirror, and display controls.

When to use both

Use Maccy when you want a dedicated clipboard workflow and Gojo when you want the surrounding notch utilities.

FAQ

Is Gojo a Maccy alternative?

They overlap on clipboard history, but Maccy is a dedicated clipboard manager. Gojo is the better fit when clipboard access is only one part of the workspace you want.

What does Maccy store?

Maccy says it keeps the history of copied content so you can navigate, search, and reuse earlier clipboard items. It also says data is stored on your computer.

Should I choose Gojo or Maccy for clipboard history?

Choose Maccy for a specialized, keyboard-first clipboard workflow. Choose Gojo when you want clipboard history alongside media, files, reminders, battery, webcam, and display controls.

Can I use Maccy and Gojo together?

Yes. Maccy can remain your dedicated clipboard manager while Gojo handles the rest of your notch workspace.
