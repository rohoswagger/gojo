Gojo vs NotchNook: focused Mac controls or a widget-heavy notch?

NotchNook turns the notch into a customizable utility center. Gojo keeps five daily Mac controls close and quiet.

Updated July 13, 20266 min readAll posts

Short answer

Choose NotchNook if you want widgets, live actions, deeper customization, notchless-screen support, and a larger device allowance. Choose Gojo if you want a focused MacBook surface for music, window snapping, clipboard history, files, and Night Shift.

  • Best for customization and widgets: NotchNook.
  • Best for a focused daily-control workflow: Gojo.
  • Both offer monthly and one-time purchase options.

Who each app is for

Choose NotchNook for a configurable utility center

NotchNook calls itself a center of utilities and emphasizes widgets, live actions, a file shelf, scrolling gestures, multiple monitors, and many options. It is the stronger fit when customization and a larger notch environment are the point.

Choose Gojo for a smaller daily loop

Gojo is built around a repeatable set of actions: control music, snap a window, recover clipboard text, stage a file, or change display warmth. It is the better fit when you want the notch useful without filling it with a large widget system.

Pricing checked July 13, 2026

Gojo is $4.99 per month or $19.99 lifetime and includes a fully unlocked 3-day trial. NotchNook's official page lists $3 per month or $25 for a one-time purchase, plus a 15-day refund window. Prices can change, so verify them before buying.
