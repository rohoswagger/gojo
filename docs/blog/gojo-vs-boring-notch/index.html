Gojo vs Boring Notch: which MacBook notch utility fits your workflow?

Boring Notch emphasizes a dynamic notch for music controls and visual feedback. Gojo is a utility-first notch workspace for media, windows, clipboard, files, calendar, battery, webcam, and display controls.

Updated July 5, 20266 min readAll posts

Short answer

Choose Boring Notch if you want an expanded notch centered on music playback controls and a visualizer. Choose Gojo if you want a utility-first notch surface that combines media controls with window snapping, clipboard history, file staging, calendar and reminders, battery, webcam mirror, and Night Shift controls.

  • Best specialist fit: a music-centered expanded notch with visual playback feedback.
  • Best Gojo fit: a utility-first notch workspace that also includes media controls.
  • Compare by workflow depth: media-only delight versus a broader set of Mac controls.

Where each app fits

Where Boring Notch is stronger

Boring Notch is the more obviously music-first choice. Its public materials emphasize playback controls, a visualizer, and making the notch feel animated and expressive.

Where Gojo is stronger

Gojo is stronger when you want the notch to become a practical control surface. It groups media with windows, clipboard, files, calendar, battery, webcam mirror, and display controls.

When to use both

Most people should choose the notch interaction style they prefer. If you test both, compare overlap in music controls, file handling, and how much visual motion you want in the notch.

Permissions and trust notes

Comparison pages are only useful if they are honest about scope. Gojo is a MacBook notch utility, not a blanket replacement for every specialist app. Before granting macOS Accessibility, Automation, Camera, Calendar, or other permissions to any utility, check whether the requested access matches the feature you plan to use.

FAQ

Boring Notch emphasizes music playback controls and a visualizer in an expanded notch. Gojo is positioned as a broader MacBook-notch control surface.

Do both apps use the MacBook notch?

Yes. Boring Notch and Gojo both treat the notch as an interaction surface.

Which app is better for media controls?

Boring Notch is the more directly music-centered choice according to its official description. Gojo also includes music controls as part of a broader toolset.
