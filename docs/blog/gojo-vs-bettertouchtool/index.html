Gojo vs BetterTouchTool: notch utility or deep Mac automation?

BetterTouchTool is a deep customization and automation workbench. Gojo is a focused notch utility for everyday controls without building your own trigger system.

Updated July 9, 20266 min readAll posts

Short answer

Choose BetterTouchTool when your priority is designing custom gestures, shortcuts, triggers, and multi-step automations across your Mac. Choose Gojo when you want a focused, notch-native place for everyday media, clipboard, file, calendar, battery, webcam, display, and window actions without building a personal automation system.

  • Best specialist fit: deep customization across gestures, keys, mice, triggers, and automations.
  • Best Gojo fit: built-in notch controls without designing your own action system.
  • Choose by appetite for configuration: automation workbench or focused notch utility.

Where each app fits

Where BetterTouchTool is stronger

BetterTouchTool is stronger when you want to design your own Mac behavior. Its official documentation supports many trigger types, chained actions, app-specific configurations, custom keyboard and pointing-device behavior, a launcher, and customizable window snapping.

Where Gojo is stronger

Gojo is stronger when the goal is immediate access rather than deep configuration. It gives the notch a concise set of media, clipboard, file, calendar, battery, webcam, display, and window controls.

When to use both

Use BetterTouchTool for the automation and input behavior you want to tailor, while using Gojo as the always-nearby notch surface for daily controls and glanceable context.

Permissions and trust notes

Comparison pages are only useful if they are honest about scope. Gojo is a MacBook notch utility, not a blanket replacement for every specialist app. Before granting macOS Accessibility, Automation, Camera, Calendar, or other permissions to any utility, check whether the requested access matches the feature you plan to use.

FAQ

Is BetterTouchTool a Gojo alternative?

Partly. Both can improve everyday Mac workflows, but BetterTouchTool focuses on configurable triggers and actions across input devices, while Gojo is a notch-centered utility.

Does BetterTouchTool include window snapping?

Yes. Its documentation says it can arrange windows through mouse use, trackpad gestures, or keyboard shortcuts, with configurable snapping behavior.

Can BetterTouchTool replace a Mac utility suite?

It can cover several utility categories, including window management, clipboard access, screenshots, and launcher features. It is best suited to people comfortable configuring behavior.

Should I use Gojo or BetterTouchTool for a MacBook notch?

Choose Gojo for a focused notch-native experience. Choose BetterTouchTool when you specifically want wider automation, gesture, shortcut, and custom-widget capabilities.
